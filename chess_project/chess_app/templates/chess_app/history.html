History of Chess

Chess is a strategic board game played between two players. It evolved from chaturanga, an older game of Indian origin, played with 16 pieces on each side. This game symbolizes a war between two kingdoms. The chess game ends in two main ways: when the King is checkmated (attacked with no escape) or when the player has no legal moves left. Like a war that continues until a ruler falls, chess goes on until one King is trapped or can't move.
